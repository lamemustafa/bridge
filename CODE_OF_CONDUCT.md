# Code of Conduct

We are committed to building a respectful, collaborative, and inclusive OSS
community. This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests,
discussions, reviews, and other project spaces.

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Keep discussions technical and focused.
- Focus on solving problems, not assigning blame.
- Do not publish private customer, contributor, credential, certificate, or
  machine data.
- Escalate security concerns through [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md), not a public
  issue.

Harassment, threats, discriminatory conduct, doxxing, and repeated disruptive
behavior are not acceptable. Maintainers may edit or remove content, lock
threads, reject contributions, or restrict participation in proportion to the
behavior and its impact.

Do not include sensitive incident details in a public issue. Use GitHub's
[reporting abuse or spam](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam)
channel for urgent platform abuse. Maintainers will review project-conduct
reports consistently, minimize access to submitted information, and avoid
retaliation against good-faith reporters.
